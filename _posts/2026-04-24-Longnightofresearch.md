This week, the Vienna BioCenter opened its doors to the public during the [Long Night of Research](https://langenachtderforschung.at/), and our zebrafish embryos were of course part of the fun. 
At the Pauli lab station kids and adults alike could test their microscopy skills and investigate zebrafish embryos at different stages of development. 
Throughout the evening and into the night, more than 1000 people came to the Vienna BioCenter. It was a fun evening explaining our research to so many enthusiastic visitors, but now we need to rest our voices. 

Thank you to our organisers **Muriel** and **Theresa** and all the enthusiastic helpers on the day!
